1) Clarify requirements (before you talk to banks)
  • Entity & footprint: country, legal entity type, expected geographies/currencies.
  • Account types: operating, payroll, tax, merchant settlement, savings/treasury.
  • Payment rails: ACH/SEPA/wires, international transfers, local clearing.
  • Controls: dual approval, RBAC, audit logs, limits, fraud monitoring.
  • Integrations: ERP/accounting, payroll provider, expense tool, payment processor.
  • Risk profile: chargeback exposure, sanctions screening, KYC/AML complexity.
2) Build a shortlist & evaluation scorecard
  • Capabilities: multi-currency, bulk payments, APIs, virtual accounts (if needed).
  • Fees: monthly fees, wires, FX spreads, minimum balances, overdraft terms.
  • Support model: relationship manager vs self-serve; support hours.
  • Security: MFA, device controls, admin-change controls, alerts, positive pay (where relevant).
  • Onboarding speed: typical KYC timeline and documentation burden.
Example weighting (edit): Controls/Security 25% • Capabilities 25% • Fees 15% • Support/SLA 15% • Integrations 10% • Onboarding 10%
3) Implementation steps (end-to-end)
  • Prepare documents: formation docs, tax IDs, proof of address, UBO list, IDs, resolutions (as needed).
  • Open accounts: authorized signers, user roles, approvals, daily limits.
  • Set controls: dual approvals, payee allowlists, alerts, admin locks, access reviews.
  • Connect systems: accounting/ERP feeds, payroll, expense, billing, payment processor.
  • Reconciliation SOP: daily cash view; weekly reconciliation; month-end close checklist.
  • Test & rollout: small payments, reversals; migrate vendors/customers; update invoice instructions.
  • Monitor: fee review, fraud incidents, exceptions, periodic bank relationship check.
Practical control baseline: require two-person approval for wires/ACH and for admin changes (adding users, raising limits, changing payment instructions).
